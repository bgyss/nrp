## Post 4 — flicker

New metric the ladder needed: frame-to-frame FLIP delta, as excess over the
reference's own motion. The proxy shot passes at 0.0019 worst excess. A
flickering baseline that per-frame PSNR rates at a healthy 30 dB fails at
0.165. Flicker is invisible to per-frame metrics. Test for it.

## Post 5 — the VFX summit and the fake convergence

8-light rig, per-light proxies, gradient-matched to a painted target.
Image-space convergence: 154.5 dB. Per-light truth: only 3 of 6 lights
actually recovered. The other 3 proxies output *exactly zero* → zero
gradient → the optimizer never touched them. The metric couldn't see it.

## Post 6 — the mystery

The pattern: all 3 dead proxies are quad lights, all 3 live ones spheres.
Same budget, same architecture. Val PSNR flagged nothing — the dead ones
*scored higher* (zero is a fine predictor of mostly-dark). This week's audit
ruled out the targets: they're measurably nonzero. The collapse is in
training.

## Post 7 — the lesson

Suspect: the paper's relative-MSE loss has a zero-collapse basin — softplus
goes dark under mostly-dark sampled targets, gradients vanish, it never
returns. But that's a hypothesis, not a finding. Meta-lesson that is a
finding: aggregate metrics can't attribute. Gate the parts, not just the sum.

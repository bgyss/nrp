## Post 1 — hook

Closed out a 6-part scale/speed pass on my Neural Render Proxies
reimplementation (Sancho et al., EGSR 2026): path tracing decoupled into a

## Post 2 — throughput + resolution

6.04x speedup on streamed training (killed a Python-loop bottleneck in the
gather step). Renders now hit 1024² at 23.0dB PSNR (kitchen) plus a second
full scene (bedroom, 21.89dB). Export/save at scale: 5.78-9.13x faster.

## Post 3 — the WebGPU bug

Found a real bug before trusting any 1024² WebGPU number: the bench
harness was JSON-encoding 9M+ floats as JS arrays and crashing the page.
Fixed with base64 + atob(). Once fixed: WebGPU still beats MPS inference
at every res tested — just not real-time yet at 1024² (11.3fps p95).

## Post 4 — the rig gate

Rebuilt the rig-additivity gate under kernel conditioning. Instead of one
pass/fail number, decomposed the result per light — an honest failure
breakdown, not a smoothed-over score.

## Post 5 — the throughput sweep

Ran the sweep everyone eventually runs: bigger batch, AMP/fp16/bf16,
torch.compile. All three lost on this hardware. Bigger batch trades
quality for speed at a bad rate; autocast is unusable on this CPU backend
(no native low-precision GEMM path); torch.compile crashes on MPS outright.

## Post 6 — why report the losses

None of that's a setback — it's the real baseline for what needs
re-measuring once this moves to a rented CUDA GPU. None of the three
levers should be assumed to "just work" there either.

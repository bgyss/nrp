Closed out a 9-extension research program on my Neural Render Proxies
reimplementation (Sancho et al., EGSR 2026) — this cycle asked a bigger question
than "does the paper replicate?": is the path-cache/proxy decoupling a building
block for a real-time neural rendering pipeline — for games, animated film, and
feature VFX?

All measured on a single Apple Silicon laptop (no CUDA), every number backed by
a committed report:

⚡ Real WebGPU in real Chrome, running the actual trained proxy as a compute
shader: 2.4e-7 parity vs PyTorch, 580 fps at 128², 107 fps at 512² — clearing
30/60 fps at every resolution the TorchScript CPU/MPS backends couldn't.

📦 Out-of-core proof at 512×512/128spp (94.2M path segments): streamed training
peaks at 574 MB resident vs a 3.35 GB monolithic cache — 14.4× lower — with a
bitwise-identical loss curve at toy scale and 32.57 dB held-out PSNR at full
scale.

🎬 A preview→draft→final quality ladder with a per-config trust verdict, proven
at 512×512 on real Mitsuba caches: 33.76/35.72 dB preview/draft vs the converged
final, and exact proxy+residual identity (max error 5.6e-17) at the approved
frame.

🔬 The honest one: dynamic geometry broke it. Warm-started proxy fine-tuning
after a geometry change reached 25.20 dB vs a 44.88 dB full-retrain reference —
19.7 dB short of the recovery target. Replay regularization closed it to
11.1 dB. Still a failure, now a *settled structural finding*, and the most
useful result of the program.

The verdict document renders all three targets "partly viable" and names each
blocker. Next up: a production track that attacks exactly those blockers — one
summit demo per audience, every rung pairing a robustness proof with a
performance target.

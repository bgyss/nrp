## Delivered Work and Measurements

- **Hardening (H2–H7).** H2 retrained all eight rig proxies so they produce
  nonzero output, but the preview additivity gate remained an honest partial:
  25.56 dB PSNR passes while SSIM 0.725 and FLIP 0.257 miss their thresholds.
  H3's texture-kernel conditioning improved the two textured-quad proxies to
  19.64/19.99 dB. H4's cached-contribution WebGPU compositing reached 1.3 ms
  p95 for RGB-only slider edits and 30.9 ms for parameter edits at 512². H5's
  real-scene invalidation mask was verified, but neither tested recovery regime
  met its 1 dB target. H6 made approval-frame residual storage 0.589× raw
  storage with no recorded quality cost; int8 remained a documented floor.
  H7 reissued the feasibility verdict against this accumulated evidence.
- **Streamed scale (S1–S3).** Batched torch gathering, multi-light shard
  passes, threaded decode, and packed shards reduced the 512² streamed run to
  166.4 s, a 6.04× end-to-end gain over the committed baseline while holding
  32.52 dB held-out PSNR. Parallel shard writing recorded 5.78× speedup for
  float64 and 9.13× for packed shards. The G-buffer scatter change improved
  exporter conversion from 3.05 s to 1.63 s (1.87×) in the recorded profile.
- **Scene scale and rig quality (S2/S4).** The streamed pipeline completed a
  1024²/32 spp kitchen run at 23.0 dB with 158.2 MB resident segment data, and
  a 512² bedroom run at 21.89 dB, clearing the 18 dB stated floor. Rebuilding
  the eight-light rig with texture-kernel proxies kept all outputs nonzero but
  still failed additivity (25.67 dB PSNR, 0.727 SSIM, 0.316 FLIP); residual
  attribution now identifies per-light fidelity as the next floor rather than
  an isolated textured-quad defect.
- **Local throughput and runtime scale (S5/S6).** The sweep retained fp32,
  eager, batch-4096 as the local default: larger batches lost quality, MPS
  autocast was a wash or unstable, and `torch.compile` failed on MPS and was a
  net CPU loss. Device/precision selection is now validated across torch
  entrypoints. At 1024², T4 WebGPU parity stayed at 1.5e-6 maximum absolute
  difference, but its 88.7 ms p95 (11.3 fps) misses the 30 fps criterion;
  common RGB rig edits remain fast at 2.2 ms p95 while parameter edits take
  219.1 ms p95.
- **Next track designed, not shipped.** The Phase 7 design moves the proxy's
  spatial encoding from 2D pixel coordinates to 3D world position plus view
  direction. R1–R6 define parity, multi-camera, novel-view, real-scene,
  WebGPU-camera, and perception-interface gates before implementation begins.
